As part two of the flagship MLB product, 2023 Topps Series 2 Baseball builds on Series 1 while adding new rookies, inserts and more to collect.

Hobby boxes again promise one hit (autograph or relic), and the Hobby Jumbo HTA boxes have one autograph, one relic and one manufactured relic.

See also: 2022 Topps Series 2 Baseball Cards

2023 Topps Series 2 Baseball Base / Parallels

As expected, 2023 Topps Series 2 Baseball extends the main checklist in Series 1, going from card #331 to #660. The 330-card base set features top rookies and veterans from across the league. This includes rookie cards of Anthony Volpe, Jordan Walker and Kodai Senga.

Retail-only parallels include Royal Blue (1:10 packs), plus the debuting Blue Foilboard (#/999) and Purple Foilboard (#/799) versions.

The Hobby-only Clear (#/100) again highlights 100 names from the base set.

Collectors can also count on another batch of the Golden Mirror SSP variation cards that feature the entire 330-card base set.

Showcasing select players from the Series 1 base set, the one-of-one Art Card sketch parallel features artwork by Luke the Cardist. These average 1:12,145 Jumbo packs.

2023 Topps Series 2 Baseball Inserts

The 2023 Topps Series 2 Baseball checklist also adds some new inserts to chase. 2023 World Baseball Classic Stars is home to 60 WBC players, while Favorite Sons revisits the path to stardom for top MLB names in their home states. Another debut is Legends of the Game, focusing on 30 all-time greats.

Returning insert lines include 1988 Topps Baseball (50 cards), 1988 Topps All-Star Baseball (50 cards) and Significant Statistics (25 cards).

The limited Heavy Lumber insert should also be back with 20 woodgrain-styled cards to honor top sluggers.

Retail fans again have exclusive Stars of MLB and Chrome Stars of MLB sets. These add Red (#/70), Black (#/25) and Platinum (1/1) parallels.

Another limited chase is the Social Media Follow Back Redemption ("follow b@ck"). Capped at 10 copies or less, these cards grant a follow on Instagram from the player in question.

2023 Topps Series 2 Baseball Autographs

Autographs take a similar approach in 2023 Topps Series 2 Baseball, including several on-card versions.

Chase signed cards in 1988 Topps Baseball Autographs, 1988 Topps All-Star Baseball Autographs, Baseball Stars Autographs, and Baseball Stars Dual Autographs (#5 or less), among others.

Select inserts come as low-numbered autographs, as well, including 2023 World Baseball Classic Stars Autographs (#/25 or less) and Legends of the Game Autographs (#/25 or less).

The premium Topps Reverence Auto Patch (#/10) cards give collectors a high-end chase.

On top of that, be on the lookout for one-of-one Cut Signatures cards that pay tribute to the legends.

2023 Topps Series 2 Baseball Relics / Auto Relics

For memorabilia fans, 2023 Topps Series 2 Baseball has 1988 Topps Baseball Relics, 1988 Topps All-Star Baseball Relics and Major League Material cards.

In addition, there are commemorative relics with manufactured relics. The Retail Value Blasters contain exclusive Father’s Day Team Patch cards with blue logo cap patches. Signed versions are limited to 25 copies or less.

Manufactured relics also include 2022 Silver Slugger Award Winners and Crowning Achievements Commemorative Patch cards. These are exclusive to Hobby and Jumbo formats.

Other possible autograph relics include the Major League Material Autographs (#/50 or less) cards.

2023 Topps Series 1 Baseball Sketch Cards / Toppers

A very tough pull, the hand-drawn Sketch and Shaped Sketch cards are only in the Hobby/Jumbo boxes.

Look for Oversized Topps Record Breakers Boxloader cards exclusively in Jumbo boxes.

These add limited parallels, numbered to 50 copies or less, plus autographs (#/10).

2023 Topps Series 1 Baseball Silver Packs Promo

Hobby and Jumbo boxes also contain the second round of the Silver Packs promotion via bonus packs. The packs offer exclusive 1988 Topps Chrome cards.

As usual, Hobby boxes have one pack and Jumbo boxes have two packs.

Release Date: June 7, 2023
Hobby Configuration: 14 cards per pack, 24 packs per box, 12 boxes per case
HTA Jumbo Configuration: 46 cards per pack, 10 packs per box, 6 boxes per case
